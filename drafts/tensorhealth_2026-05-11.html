Official Labor Data Confirms AI Is Reducing White-Collar Employment in Junior Roles (Financial Times)

New research from Federal Reserve economists shows official labor statistics now corroborate earlier findings that AI is reducing employment for certain white-collar workers, particularly junior software developers and contractors. The data reveals roughly half a million fewer coders are working than pre-LLM trends predicted. Economists explain this through a "bundles" framework: AI eliminates weakly-bundled junior roles focused on discrete coding tasks, while enhancing tightly-bundled senior positions where coding is integrated with domain expertise and judgment.

Researchers developed a voice-driven AI system that guides non-medical users through expert-level stroke evaluations using natural conversation and smartphone video capture. Testing with non-medical volunteers showed 84% accuracy identifying stroke signs and 75% detection of likely large vessel occlusions, though expert review was still needed for confident treatment decisions.
