AI-SHIFT 🌀

AI Is Shrinking Junior Coding Jobs — and the 'Task Bundle' Theory Explains Why (Financial Times)

New research from Federal Reserve economists, corroborated by official US labour force survey data, estimates roughly half a million fewer coders are working today than pre-LLM trends would have predicted. The key insight is that jobs are "bundles" of tasks: junior developers and contractors hold weak bundles — mainly writing code to spec — making their roles easy to automate away. Senior developers, whose coding is tightly intertwined with domain expertise and judgment, see AI act as a multiplier rather than a replacement.

Top Medical-AI Papers🧬

Voice-guided Orchestrated Intelligence for Clinical Evaluation (VOICE): A Voice AI Agent System for Prehospital Stroke Assessment (arXiv)

Researchers built a voice-driven AI system that guides non-medical users through expert-level stroke evaluations using natural conversation and smartphone video capture. The system correctly identified 84% of individual stroke signs and detected 75% of likely large vessel occlusions, though it misclassified 2 of 3 non-stroke cases, highlighting the need for human oversight.

a2z-1 for Multi-Disease Detection in Abdomen-Pelvis CT: External Validation and Performance Analysis Across 21 Conditions (arXiv)

The authors evaluated a2z-1, an AI model for detecting 21 actionable findings in abdomen-pelvis CT scans, across large-scale retrospective and external validation datasets. The model achieved an average AUC of 0.931 internally and 0.923 across two external health systems, with strong performance on conditions like small bowel obstruction (AUC 0.958) and acute pancreatitis (AUC 0.961).
